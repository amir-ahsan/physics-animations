What you are looking at

The Carnot cycle is the most efficient possible heat engine between two temperatures. It runs two isotherms (heat in at T_hot, heat out at T_cold) joined by two adiabats, forming a closed loop on the P–V diagram. The enclosed area is the net work per cycle, and the efficiency is η = 1 − T_cold/T_hot — set only by the two reservoir temperatures.
