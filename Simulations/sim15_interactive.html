What you are looking at

A ball is launched straight up or down with initial velocity v₀ from height y₀, then moves under gravity alone: y(t) = y₀ + v₀t − ½gt² and v(t) = v₀ − gt. It rises, slows, stops for an instant at the top (t = v₀/g), then falls back. Press Play and watch the height and velocity graphs together with the moving ball.

Green arrow = velocity (up when positive, flipping to down after the peak).

Height & velocity vs. time

Blue = height y(t) (a parabola); green = velocity v(t) (a straight line). The dot marks the present instant; the peak is where velocity crosses zero.

What each plot shows

  • Height y(t) — rises then falls; slope is the velocity.
  • Velocity v(t) — falls at a constant rate g; zero at the top.

Questions to explore

  • Predictbefore you drag At the highest point, what is the velocity? What is the acceleration there?
