What you are looking at

An isobaric process holds pressure constant — think of a gas under a free-floating piston of fixed weight. As you add heat the gas expands at constant P, doing work W = PΔV. The path on the P–V diagram is a horizontal line, and the work is simply the rectangle beneath it. Some of the heat does work; the rest raises the temperature.

Constant-pressure expansion

work W = PΔV = –
heat Q = nC_pΔT = –
ΔU = Q − W = –
The piston rises so pressure stays fixed; the gas does work lifting it while also warming up.
