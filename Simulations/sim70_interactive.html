What you are looking at

Every warm object glows with a characteristic spectrum set only by its temperature. The Planck curve shifts and grows with T: hotter objects peak at shorter wavelengths (Wien's law, λ_peak ∝ 1/T) and radiate far more total power (Stefan–Boltzmann, P ∝ T⁴). This is why a heated iron goes from red to orange to white as it gets hotter.
