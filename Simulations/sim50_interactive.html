What you are looking at

A series RLC circuit driven by an AC source has a current amplitude I₀ = V₀/√(R² + (ωL − 1/ωC)²). It peaks at the resonant frequency ω₀ = 1/√(LC), where the inductive and capacitive reactances cancel and only R limits the current. Smaller resistance makes a taller, sharper peak — a higher quality factor Q.
