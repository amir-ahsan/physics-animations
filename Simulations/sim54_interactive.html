What you are looking at

An isochoric (constant-volume) process locks the piston in place. Since the volume never changes, the gas does no work (W = PΔV = 0), and all the heat added goes straight into internal energy: Q = ΔU = nC_vΔT. On the P–V diagram the path is a vertical line — pressure rises as you heat, but the gas can't expand.

Sealed rigid container

work W = PΔV = 0.00 (fixed V)

The lid is bolted down: molecules speed up and hit harder (pressure climbs), but the walls never move, so no work is done.

P–V diagram (vertical path)

A vertical line at constant V from P_i to P_f. No area under a vertical line means no work.
