What you are looking at

A mass on a spring obeys Hooke's law F = −kx: the force always pulls back toward equilibrium, growing with displacement. The stored energy is U = ½kx², a parabola. Released from amplitude A, the mass oscillates as x(t) = A cos(ωt) with ω = √(k/m). Watch the force line, the energy bowl, and the moving mass together.

The oscillating mass

force F = −kx = – N
stored energy U = – J
ω = √(k/m) = – rad/s
period T = 2π/ω = – s
The mass slides through equilibrium fastest and stops momentarily at the turning points x = ±A.

Force & energy vs. displacement

Red line = restoring force F(x) = −kx; blue parabola = potential energy U(x) = ½kx². The dot marks the mass's current displacement.

What each plot shows

  • Restoring force F(x) = −kx — straight line through the origin.
  • Potential energy U(x) = ½kx² — a bowl centred on equilibrium.

Play with the parameters

Stiffer spring → faster oscillation.
Heavier mass → slower.
