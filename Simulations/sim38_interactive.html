What you are looking at

Two batteries drive a circuit with three branches meeting at a junction. The junction rule says current in = current out (I₃ = I₁+I₂), and the loop rule says voltage changes around any closed loop sum to zero. Solving the two loop equations gives all three branch currents. A negative current just means it actually flows opposite to the arrow you drew.

The two-loop circuit

I₁ (left loop) = – A
I₂ (right loop) = – A
I₃ = I₁+I₂ (middle) = – A
Both batteries push current toward the central R₃ branch; the junction rule ties the three currents together.
