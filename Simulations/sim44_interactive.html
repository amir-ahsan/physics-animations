What you are looking at

Ampère's law, ∮B·dl = μ₀I_enc, gives the field of a thick wire carrying uniform current. Inside the wire only the enclosed fraction of current counts, so B ∝ r and grows linearly. Outside, all the current is enclosed and B ∝ 1/r. The two pieces meet smoothly at the wire's surface, where B is largest.

Field vs. radius
