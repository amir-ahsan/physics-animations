What you are looking at

A resistor R and capacitor C in series set the pace of charging and discharging. The capacitor voltage follows V = V₀(1 − e−t/τ) while charging and V = V₀ e−t/τ while discharging, where the time constant is τ = RC. The current does the opposite — largest at the start, then fading. Press Play, switch between charging and discharging, and see the capacitor plates fill and empty.

What each plot shows

  • Capacitor voltage V(t) — rises to V₀ (charging) or decays to 0 (discharging).
  • Current I(t) — largest at t = 0, then fades; negative while discharging.
  • τ = RC — one time constant.
