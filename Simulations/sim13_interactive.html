What you are looking at

Light shining on a metal can knock electrons loose — but only if each photon carries enough energy. Below a threshold frequency f₀ = φ/h nothing comes out, no matter how bright the light. Above it, the maximum kinetic energy is Kmax = hf − φ. Brighter light (higher intensity) ejects more electrons but does not give them more energy. Cross the threshold and watch emission switch on.
