What you are looking at

Gas molecules don't all move at the same speed — they follow the Maxwell–Boltzmann distribution. The curve shows how many molecules have each speed. Raise the temperature and the whole distribution shifts right and flattens (faster, more spread out); use heavier molecules and it shifts left (slower). The peak is the most probable speed v_p = √(2kT/m).

Speed distribution f(v)

f(v) ∝ v²e^(−mv²/2kT). Hotter or lighter gas → peak moves right; the area under the curve is fixed.
