What you are looking at

A satellite in a circular orbit is held by gravity F = GMm/r², which provides exactly the centripetal force it needs. Setting the two equal gives the orbital speed v = √(GM/r) and period T = 2π√(r³/GM) — Kepler's third law. Bigger orbits are slower and take longer. Change the planet's mass and the orbit radius and watch.
