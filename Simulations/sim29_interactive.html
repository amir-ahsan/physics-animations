What you are looking at

Apply the same torque τ to three objects with the same mass M and radius R — a hoop, a solid disk, and a solid sphere. Rotational inertia is I = cMR² with shape factor c = 1 (hoop), ½ (disk), ⅞ (sphere). The angular acceleration is α = τ/I, so the sphere (smallest I) spins up fastest and the hoop slowest. Same push, different response.

Three objects, same torque

α sphere = – rad/s²
α disk = – rad/s²
α hoop = – rad/s²
Same torque on each — watch the sphere outrun the disk, and the disk outrun the hoop.

Angle turned vs. time

θ(t) = ½αt². The steepest curve (sphere) has the smallest rotational inertia.

What each plot shows

  • Sphere (I = ⅞MR²) — spins up fastest.
  • Disk (I = ½MR²).
  • Hoop (I = MR²) — slowest.
