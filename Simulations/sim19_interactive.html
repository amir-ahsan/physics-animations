What you are looking at

A block m₁ on a table is pulled by a cord over a pulley to a hanging block m₂. The hanging weight drives the system; friction under m₁ resists. Both share one acceleration a = (m₂g − μₖm₁g)/(m₁ + m₂), with cord tension T = m₁(a + μₖg). If the hanging weight cannot beat friction, nothing moves. Treat the two blocks as one system, then check each alone.
