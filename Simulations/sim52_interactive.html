What you are looking at

An isothermal process holds temperature constant, so the gas moves along one hyperbola PV = nRT. Because the internal energy of an ideal gas depends only on T, ΔU = 0, and every joule of heat added becomes work done by the gas: Q = W = nRT ln(V_f/V_i). The shaded area under the curve is that work.

Expanding at constant T

Heat flows in exactly as fast as the gas does work, keeping temperature — and internal energy — unchanged.
