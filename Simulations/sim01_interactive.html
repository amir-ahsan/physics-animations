What you are looking at

A particle moves in one dimension with constant acceleration. Its position, velocity, and acceleration obey x(t) = xᵢ + vᵢt + ½at², v(t) = vᵢ + at, and a = constant. The three stacked graphs (position on top, velocity in the middle, acceleration at the bottom) share the same time axis, and the schematic shows the actual particle on its track. Watch how the slope of one graph becomes the value of the next.

The particle on its track

x(t) = – m
v(t) = – m/s
a = – m/s²
turns around at t = – s
Green arrow = velocity (points the way it moves, length ∝ speed). Red arrow = acceleration.

Position, velocity & acceleration vs. time

Top band = position, middle = velocity, bottom = acceleration (the lower two are shifted down onto their own baselines). The dots mark the present instant.

What each plot shows

  • Position x(t) — a parabola for constant acceleration.
  • Velocity v(t) — a straight line; its slope is the acceleration.
  • Acceleration a — a flat line (constant).
