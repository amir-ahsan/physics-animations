What you are looking at

An adiabatic process exchanges no heat (Q = 0) — the cylinder is perfectly insulated or the change is fast. The gas follows PVγ = constant, a steeper curve than an isotherm. With no heat in, any work done comes straight out of internal energy: expand and the gas cools; compress and it heats. This is why a bicycle pump warms up.
