What you are looking at

Radioactive nuclei decay at random, but in bulk they follow N(t) = N₀e−λt. Each half-life t½ = ln2/λ the population halves — after one half-life half remain, after two a quarter, and so on. The decay constant λ sets the pace: larger λ means faster decay and a shorter half-life.
