Learning goal

Synchronize a moving particle with position-time, velocity-time, and acceleration-time graphs for constant acceleration.

Suggested window: x-axis: -1 to 12; y-axis: -50 to 35. Keep grid on. The three graph panels are vertically offset.

Paste-ready Desmos expressions

Desmos Notebook is built in the web editor from text, expressions, sliders, and graph items — add a 2D graph from the Notebook sidebar and paste these lines into it, one per line. Create sliders when Desmos offers; press play beside t to animate.

T=10
t=0
x_i=0
v_i=4
a=-0.6
O_v=-18
O_a=-32
X(s)=x_i+v_i s+0.5 a s^2
V(s)=v_i+a s
A(s)=a
(s,X(s)){0<=s<=T}
(s,V(s)+O_v){0<=s<=T}
(s,A(s)+O_a){0<=s<=T}
P=(t,X(t))
Q=(X(t),-45)
(X(s),-45){0<=s<=t}
y=O_v
y=O_a
(t,X(t))
(t,V(t)+O_v)
(t,A(t)+O_a)

Sliders

  • t: 0 to T, step 0.05, animate forward/repeat
  • x_i: -10 to 10, step 0.5
  • v_i: -10 to 10, step 0.5
  • a: -5 to 5, step 0.1

Tutorial prompts — Predict · Manipulate · Explain · Calculate · Extend

  • Predict: Before animating, decide whether the particle will speed up, slow down, reverse, or keep moving in one direction.
  • Manipulate: Change a from positive to negative while holding v_i fixed. Watch the velocity graph and particle motion.
  • Explain: Explain why the slope of X(s) equals V(s), and why the slope of V(s) equals A(s).
  • Calculate: For v_i=4 m/s and a=-0.6 m/s^2, calculate the time when the particle momentarily stops and compare with the graph.
  • Extend: Ask students to design values of x_i, v_i, and a so the object returns to its starting point at t=8 s.

Building the Notebook, step by step

  1. Open the Desmos Notebook editor (it combines text, expressions, sliders, and graphs; graphs are added and edited through the Notebook sidebar).
  2. Add the title and learning goal as a text item.
  3. Add the slider variables as expression items; use Variable Options → Add Slider with the ranges above.
  4. Add a 2D graph item and paste the expression list into its sidebar.
  5. Set the graph window to the suggested bounds.
  6. Add the Predict → Manipulate → Explain → Calculate → Extend prompts as text items.
  7. Preview, save, and share — then paste the share link into PUBLISHED_URL at the top of this page so the tutorial button goes straight to it.
