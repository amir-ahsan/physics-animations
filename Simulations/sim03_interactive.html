What you are looking at

A block of mass m rests on a plane tilted at angle θ. Gravity W = mg splits into a piece pressing into the surface, N = mg cosθ, and a piece pulling it down the slope, mg sinθ. While the block is at rest, static friction rises to exactly cancel that pull (up to its limit μₛN), so it never exceeds mg sinθ. Once mg sinθ > μₛN the block breaks free and slides, and kinetic friction μₖN takes over, giving a = g(sinθ − μₖ cosθ).

Driving force vs. friction, as the angle changes

Where the red curve (gravity along the slope) rises above the blue curve (maximum static friction), the block breaks free; beyond that, green kinetic friction resists the slide.

What each plot shows

  • Gravity along the slope mg sinθ — the force trying to slide the block.
  • Maximum static friction μₛmg cosθ — the most the surface can hold before sliding.
  • Kinetic friction μₖmg cosθ — resists motion once the block is sliding.
