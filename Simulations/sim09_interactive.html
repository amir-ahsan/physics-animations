What you are looking at

A gas is taken around a closed rectangular cycle on a pressure–volume diagram. Each horizontal leg is isobaric, where the gas does work W = PΔV; each vertical leg is isochoric, where W = 0. The net work done by the gas in one loop is the area enclosed, W = (P⁃ − Pₗ)(V⁃ − Vₗ), and a clockwise loop does positive net work. Walk the piston around the cycle (A→B→C→D→A) and watch the state point trace the rectangle.

The cycle on a P–V diagram

Shaded rectangle = the cycle; its area is the net work. The thick highlighted edge is the process the gas is on right now; the dot is the current state.

What each plot shows

  • Cycle A→B→C→D — the enclosed area is the net work.
  • Isobars (horizontal edges): work W = P·ΔV — positive on the top, negative on the bottom.
  • Isochors (vertical edges): volume fixed, so W = 0.

Play with the parameters

0=A, 1=B, 2=C, 3=D, 4=back to A.
