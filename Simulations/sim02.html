Learning goal

Model ideal projectile motion and display trajectory, instantaneous position, and component velocities.

Suggested window: x-axis: -5 to 80; y-axis: -5 to 45. Adjust depending on v_0 and launch height.

Paste-ready Desmos expressions

Desmos Notebook is built in the web editor from text, expressions, sliders, and graph items — add a 2D graph from the Notebook sidebar and paste these lines into it, one per line. Create sliders when Desmos offers; press play beside t to animate.

g=9.8
v_0=25
D=45
r=D*pi/180
y_0=0
T=(v_0 sin(r)+sqrt((v_0 sin(r))^2+2 g y_0))/g
t=0
X(s)=v_0 cos(r) s
Y(s)=y_0+v_0 sin(r) s-0.5 g s^2
v_x=v_0 cos(r)
v_y(s)=v_0 sin(r)-g s
(X(s),Y(s)){0<=s<=T}
P=(X(t),Y(t))
(X(s),Y(s)){0<=s<=t}
H=y_0+(v_0 sin(r))^2/(2g)
R=v_0 cos(r) T
(R,0)
(X(t)+u*0.15*v_x,Y(t)){0<=u<=1}
(X(t),Y(t)+u*0.15*v_y(t)){0<=u<=1}
y=H{0<=x<=R}

Sliders

  • t: 0 to T, step 0.02, animate forward
  • v_0: 5 to 40, step 1
  • D: 0 to 90, step 1
  • y_0: 0 to 30, step 1

Tutorial prompts — Predict · Manipulate · Explain · Calculate · Extend

  • Predict: For launch and landing at the same height, predict whether 30 degrees or 60 degrees gives a larger range.
  • Manipulate: Set y_0=0 and compare complementary launch angles such as 30 and 60 degrees.
  • Explain: Explain why horizontal velocity stays constant while vertical velocity changes linearly.
  • Calculate: Use the graph values to calculate range and maximum height for v_0=25 m/s and D=45 degrees.
  • Extend: Turn on a nonzero launch height and ask why complementary angles no longer give equal ranges.

Building the Notebook, step by step

  1. Open the Desmos Notebook editor (it combines text, expressions, sliders, and graphs; graphs are added and edited through the Notebook sidebar).
  2. Add the title and learning goal as a text item.
  3. Add the slider variables as expression items; use Variable Options → Add Slider with the ranges above.
  4. Add a 2D graph item and paste the expression list into its sidebar.
  5. Set the graph window to the suggested bounds.
  6. Add the Predict → Manipulate → Explain → Calculate → Extend prompts as text items.
  7. Preview, save, and share — then paste the share link into PUBLISHED_URL at the top of this page so the tutorial button goes straight to it.
