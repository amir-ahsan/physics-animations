What you are looking at

An X-ray photon that scatters off a free electron loses energy, so its wavelength increases by Δλ = (h/m_ec)(1 − cos θ). The shift depends only on the scattering angle, not the incoming wavelength — direct evidence that light carries momentum like a particle. Back-scatter (θ = 180°) gives the biggest shift; forward scatter gives none.
