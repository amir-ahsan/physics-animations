What you are looking at

A light beam of length L rests on two supports at its ends. A load of weight W sits a distance x from the left support. For the beam to balance, both the forces and the torques must sum to zero, giving the support reactions NL = W(L−x)/L and NR = Wx/L. Slide the load and watch how the weight shifts between the two supports.

Loaded beam

left support N₄ = – N
right support Nᵣ = – N
check: N₄ + Nᵣ = – N = W
Blue arrows = support reactions (they grow as the load moves toward that support); red = the load's weight.

Support forces vs. load position

As the load slides right, the right support takes more and the left takes less — always adding to W.

What each plot shows

  • NL — falls as the load moves right.
  • NR — rises as the load moves right.
