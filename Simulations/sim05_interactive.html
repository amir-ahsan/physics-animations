What you are looking at

Two point charges q₁ and q₂ sit on the x-axis. Each creates a potential V = k q/r, and the total potential is the sum of the two. The curves are equipotentials (lines of constant V). At any sample point the electric field is the sum of the two Coulomb fields, and it always points perpendicular to the equipotentials, from high to low potential. Move the sample point and the charges to explore superposition.
