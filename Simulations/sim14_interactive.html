What you are looking at

A quantum particle trapped in a box of width L can only have certain quantized energies Eₙ ∝ n²/L². Its wavefunction must vanish at both walls, so only standing waves with a whole number of half-wavelengths fit — giving n − 1 internal nodes. The probability of finding the particle is the wavefunction squared. Change n and L to see the levels spread out and the wiggles multiply.
