What you are looking at

A curved mirror forms images according to 1/f = 1/d_o + 1/d_i, with magnification m = −d_i/d_o. A concave mirror (f > 0) can make real, inverted images or, up close, magnified virtual ones. A convex mirror (f < 0) always makes small, upright, virtual images — the wide-angle "objects are closer than they appear" mirror. Move the object and watch the image jump.
