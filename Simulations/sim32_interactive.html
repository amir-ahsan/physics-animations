What you are looking at

Each charge sets up an electric field E = kq/r² that points away from a positive charge and toward a negative one. Where two charges overlap, the total field is the vector sum of the two. The grid of arrows shows the field's direction and strength everywhere; the graph shows the field along the axis, where it can even reach zero between two like charges.

Field-vector map

arrows point along the local field
longer arrow = stronger field
Blue arrows = electric field direction; away from + charges, toward − charges. The pattern is superposition in action.

Field along the axis

Eₓ along the line through the charges (positive = pointing right). It blows up near each charge and can cross zero between like charges.

What each plot shows

  • Eₓ(x) — the field's x-component along the axis.
