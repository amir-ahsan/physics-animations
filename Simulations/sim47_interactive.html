What you are looking at

Move a bar magnet toward or away from a loop and the changing flux induces a current. Lenz's law says that current always flows so its own magnetic field opposes the change: approach the loop and it pushes back; pull away and it pulls to hold on. The induced emf is ε = −dΦ/dt — the minus sign is Lenz's law.
