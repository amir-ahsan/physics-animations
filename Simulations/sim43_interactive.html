What you are looking at

A long straight wire wraps circular magnetic field lines around itself, with strength B = μ₀I/(2πr). The field circles the wire (right-hand rule: thumb along the current, fingers curl the way B points) and falls off as 1/r with distance. More current means stronger loops; move away and the field fades.
