What you are looking at

A block of mass m starts with speed vᵢ and is pushed by a position-dependent force F(x) = F₀ − kx. The work done is the area under the F–x graph, W = ∫F dx, and the work-energy theorem says that area equals the change in kinetic energy, W = ½mvₓ² − ½mvᵢ². Slide the probe (or press Play) to sweep the push and watch the shaded work build up and the kinetic-energy meter respond.
