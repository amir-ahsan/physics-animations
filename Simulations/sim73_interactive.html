What you are looking at

A quantum particle can pass through a barrier taller than its energy — something impossible in classical physics. Inside the barrier the wavefunction decays exponentially, so a little of it leaks out the far side. The transmission probability falls off sharply with barrier width and height, roughly T ≈ e−2κL with κ = √(2m(U−E))/ℏ. Thin barriers tunnel easily; thick ones almost never.
