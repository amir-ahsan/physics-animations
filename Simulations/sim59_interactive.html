What you are looking at

Most materials expand when heated. A rod of length L₀ grows by ΔL = αL₀ΔT, where α is the coefficient of linear expansion. The effect is small but real — it's why bridges have expansion joints and why a tight metal lid loosens under hot water. Longer rods, bigger temperature changes, and larger α all mean more expansion.
