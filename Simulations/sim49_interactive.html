What you are looking at

A charged capacitor connected to an inductor sloshes energy back and forth forever (with no resistance). The charge oscillates as q(t) = Q₀cos ωt at angular frequency ω = 1/√(LC), and the current I = −ωQ₀sin ωt is a quarter-cycle out of phase. Energy trades between the capacitor's electric field and the inductor's magnetic field — the electrical analogue of a mass on a spring.
