Learning goal

Visualize superposition using equipotential curves and a movable electric-field vector sample point.

Suggested window: x-axis: -8 to 8; y-axis: -6 to 6. Turn on grid. Avoid placing the sample point exactly on a charge.

Sliders

  • q_1: -3 to 3, step 0.5
  • q_2: -3 to 3, step 0.5
  • x_1, x_2: -5 to 5, step 0.5
  • y_1, y_2: -4 to 4, step 0.5
  • V_0: 0.1 to 2, step 0.1
  • x_s: -7 to 7, step 0.1
  • y_s: -5 to 5, step 0.1
  • s_c: 0.1 to 3, step 0.1

Tutorial prompts — Predict · Manipulate · Explain · Calculate · Extend

  • Predict: For equal and opposite charges, predict the direction of the field at the midpoint.
  • Manipulate: Move the sample point S and observe how the field vector relates to the equipotential curves.
  • Explain: Explain why electric field vectors are perpendicular to equipotential curves.
  • Calculate: Choose a point on the x-axis and calculate E_x from Coulomb’s law, then compare with the displayed field vector.
  • Extend: Change q_2 from negative to positive and compare dipole-like and like-charge equipotentials.

Building the Notebook, step by step

  1. Open the Desmos Notebook editor (it combines text, expressions, sliders, and graphs; graphs are added and edited through the Notebook sidebar).
  2. Add the title and learning goal as a text item.
  3. Add the slider variables as expression items; use Variable Options → Add Slider with the ranges above.
  4. Add a 2D graph item and paste the expression list into its sidebar.
  5. Set the graph window to the suggested bounds.
  6. Add the Predict → Manipulate → Explain → Calculate → Extend prompts as text items.
  7. Preview, save, and share — then paste the share link into PUBLISHED_URL at the top of this page so the tutorial button goes straight to it.
