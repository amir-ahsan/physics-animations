What you are looking at

A box of mass m on a rough floor is pushed right by F₁ and left by F₂. Kinetic friction fₖ = μₖmg opposes any sliding. Newton's second law then sets the acceleration: a = (F₁ − F₂ − fₖ) / m. If the applied forces cannot overcome friction, the box stays put. Change the forces and mass and watch the free-body diagram and the resulting motion.

Resulting motion (from rest)

Blue = position x(t) = ½at²; green = velocity v(t) = at. A flat pair of lines means the box is not moving.

What each plot shows

  • Position x(t) — a parabola under constant a.
  • Velocity v(t) — a straight line of slope a.
