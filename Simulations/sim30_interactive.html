What you are looking at

A mass on a spring oscillates as x(t) = A cos(ωt + φ) with ω = √(k/m). Differentiating gives velocity v = −Aωsin(ωt+φ) and acceleration a = −Aω²cos(ωt+φ) = −ω²x. Velocity leads position by a quarter cycle; acceleration is exactly opposite the displacement. Watch all three sinusoids and the moving mass.

The oscillating mass

position x = – m
velocity v = – m/s

Position, velocity & acceleration vs. time

Blue = x(t), green = v(t), red = a(t). Notice velocity peaks a quarter-cycle before position, and acceleration is the mirror of position.

What each plot shows

  • Position x(t) = A cos(ωt+φ).
  • Velocity v(t) — a quarter-cycle ahead.
  • Acceleration a(t) = −ω²x — opposite the displacement.
