What you are looking at

A projectile is launched with speed v₀ at angle θ from height y₀. The motion splits into two independent pieces: horizontal velocity vₓ = v₀cosθ stays constant, while vertical velocity vₖ = v₀sinθ − gt changes under gravity. Together they trace a parabola. Press Play to fly the shot and watch the velocity split into a fixed horizontal arrow and a shrinking-then-growing vertical arrow.

Questions to explore

  • Predictbefore you drag For launch and landing at the same height, which gives more range — 30° or 60°?
  • Manipulateθ, y₀=0 With y₀ = 0, compare complementary angles such as 30° and 60°. What do you notice about the ranges?
  • Explainthe arrows Why does the horizontal velocity stay constant while the vertical velocity changes linearly?
  • Calculatev₀, θ For v₀ = 25 m/s and θ = 45°, calculate the range and maximum height, then check them against the readouts.
  • Extendy₀ Turn on a nonzero launch height. Why do complementary angles no longer give equal ranges?
