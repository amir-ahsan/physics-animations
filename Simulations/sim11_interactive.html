What you are looking at

Light of wavelength λ passes through slits of width a separated by d, and lands on a screen a distance L away. A single slit spreads the light into a broad diffraction envelope whose central peak narrows as the slit widens. Two slits add closely spaced interference fringes inside that envelope. Change a and d separately to see the envelope and the fringes respond independently.

Play with the parameters

Narrower slit → wider central peak.
Larger separation → finer fringes.
