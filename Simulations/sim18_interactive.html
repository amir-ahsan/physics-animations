What you are looking at

Two masses m₁ and m₂ hang from a light cord over a frictionless pulley. The heavier one falls, the lighter rises, and both share one acceleration a = (m₁ − m₂)g / (m₁ + m₂) with a single cord tension T = 2m₁m₂g / (m₁ + m₂). The bigger the mass difference, the bigger the acceleration. Press Play and watch.

The machine

acceleration a = – m/s²
tension T = – N
weights: m₁g = – N, m₂g = – N
The heavier mass descends; the tension is the same throughout the single cord.

Velocity of the system vs. time

The speed grows linearly at rate a; a bigger mass difference gives a steeper line.

What each plot shows

  • Speed v(t) = a t of both masses.

Questions to explore

  • Calculatem₁, m₂ For m₁ = 3 kg, m₂ = 2 kg, g = 9.8, find a and T.
